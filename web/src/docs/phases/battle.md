## Overview

The battle phase is where you play a quick mini-game of magic with a few modified rules.

## Magic Rule Modifications

- Each player starts with 10 life
- Libraries start empty, and you cannot lose the game due to not being able to draw cards.
- Conceding is not allowed because [Treasure tokens](doc:game-pieces#treasures) created during a battle persist afterwards.
- A draw is considered a loss for both players.

## Before the Battle Starts

- All basic lands selected during the [build phase](doc:build) start on the battlefield untapped.
- All [treasures](doc:game-pieces#treasures) a player has at the beginning of the battle phase will start on their battlefield.
- All [poison counters](doc:game-pieces#poison) a player has is attached to them during the battle.
- The player with the most [poison counters](doc:game-pieces#poison) gets their choice of play/draw. If players are tied, the choosing player is selected randomly. In the finals, after the first game, the loser of the previous game chooses instead.
- Each player's starting hand and sideboard become exactly what was selected during the [build phase](doc:build).

## After the Battle Ends

- The losing player(s) get N [poison counters](doc:game-pieces#poison), where N is 1 + the number of [upgrades](doc:game-pieces#upgrades) their opponent has applied to cards in their submitted battle pool: their submitted hand, plus their selected companion if they have one. If you are not playing with upgrades, N is Fibonacci(stage - 2).
- Each player keeps up to 5 [treasures](doc:game-pieces#treasures) that are still on their battlefield and exiles the rest.

## Controls

- You can drag and drop cards between any zones, including your opponent's zones in order to make casting cards like Reanimate easier to resolve.
- Click "actions" on the bottom left to see what actions you can take.
  - If a card is selected, some extra actions will appear.
  - Right clicking on a card will also bring up the action options for that card.
- Click "submit" on the bottom right when you know the result of the game to move onto the next phase.
