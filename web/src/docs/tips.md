## Global

- Track your economy. If a line wins a battle but burns multiple treasures, it might be better to lose.
- Keep your next stage in mind. A card that is mediocre now can be excellent when your hand size increases.
- Look at your opponents' details in the sidebar: poison totals, treasure totals, known upgrades, and recent cards cast.

## Draft

- If it looks like you are going to lose a lot, consider rolling to get back into the game.
- Always consider not just your current hand, but what your hand for next stage will be.
- Draft with upgrades in mind so you can have huge power spikes at the next stage.
- Small, incremental, improvements are not enough. Aim for a broken hand with disruption.

## Build

- For 3-card hands, strongly prioritize the ability to produce treasures if you can.
- Instants and flash are better than normal. Sometimes with them it's optimal to choose to draw.
- Check the sidebar for what your opponents are doing and tailor your build to beat them.
- If it looks like you can't beat them, you need to produce treasures to roll for a different strategy.
- Remember, player with the most poison chooses play or draw. Given who you may play against, this can drastically affect your build.

## Battle

- Because draws count as losses for both players, be very mindful of just trading resources.
- Using treasures to win battles is much better in later stages than in early stages.
- If you have a repeatable treasure producer, try to keep it alive while exhausting your opponents resources.

## Reward

- Be very careful which upgrade you choose. You don't need to apply it immediately, but the longer you wait, the weaker you are.
- Applying upgrades are permanent. So don't choose one that will go onto a card you don't plan on playing in the lategame.
