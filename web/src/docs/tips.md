## Global

- Track your economy first. If a line wins a battle but burns all your treasures, make sure the payoff is worth losing future rerolls.
- Keep your next stage in mind. A card that is mediocre now can be excellent when your hand size increases.
- Watch public information constantly: poison totals, treasure totals, known upgrades, and recent cards cast.
- Build with flexibility when pairings are uncertain. Narrow hate cards are strongest when your likely opponents are obvious.
- Treat every battle as both a result and a scouting opportunity for the next build.

## Draft

- Spend treasures aggressively when your current pack has no realistic upgrades to your pool.
- Prioritize cards that solve multiple problems over niche cards that only shine in one matchup.
- When close to the 5-treasure post-battle cap, convert excess treasure into card quality now.
- Draft with your known upgrades in mind so you keep enough legal/nameable targets.
- If your pool lacks a clear plan, draft for consistency first (curve, interaction, mana quality).

## Build

- Start from your win condition, then backfill support cards and interaction.
- Use the Opponents panel before locking your hand; hidden information is limited, public information is high-value.
- Re-check your basic land choices after final hand swaps so your mana supports your actual opening line.
- If your hand is reactive, choosing draw can be stronger than forcing play.
- Only apply upgrades when the target is likely to remain core to your plan for future stages.

## Battle

- Plan your treasure usage before turn one; panic-spending mid-combat usually lowers EV.
- Because draws count as losses, identify your "must-push" turn early and commit resources there.
- Preserve board clarity when resolving complex effects; cleaner states reduce submission mistakes.
- If you are ahead, play to avoid high-variance lines that can collapse into a draw/loss.
- If you are behind, force interaction windows where your outs remain live.

## Reward

- Use reward transitions to reset priorities: economy stability, matchup adaptation, and stage scaling.
- Round 3 decisions are long-tail decisions; they shape multiple future battles, not just the next one.
- When upgrades are enabled, choose upgrade packages that stay relevant across many different hands.
- If your poison is high, favor lines that increase hand reliability over speculative spikes.
- Enter the next draft with a clear shopping list of missing effects so rerolls are decisive.
