## Global

- Track your economy. If a line wins a battle but burns multiple [treasures](doc:game-pieces#treasures), it might be better to lose.
- Keep your next stage in mind. A card that is mediocre now can be excellent when your hand size grows during [round wrap-up](doc:reward).
- Look at your opponents' details in the [sidebar](doc:controls): [poison](doc:game-pieces#poison) totals, [treasure](doc:game-pieces#treasures) totals, known [upgrades](doc:game-pieces#upgrades), and recent cards cast.

## Draft

- If it looks like you are going to lose a lot, consider [rolling](doc:game-pieces#treasures) to get back into the game.
- Always consider not just your current hand, but what your hand for next stage will be.
- Draft with [upgrades](doc:game-pieces#upgrades) in mind so you can have huge power spikes at the next stage.
- Small, incremental, improvements are not enough. Aim for a broken hand with disruption.

## Build

- For 3-card hands, strongly prioritize the ability to produce [treasures](doc:game-pieces#treasures) if you can.
- Instants and flash are better than normal. Sometimes with them it's optimal to choose to draw.
- Check the sidebar for what your opponents are doing and tailor your build to beat them.
- If it looks like you can't beat them, you need to produce treasures to roll for a different strategy.
- Remember, player with the most [poison](doc:game-pieces#poison) chooses play or draw. Given who you may play against, this can drastically affect your build.

## Battle

- Because [draws count as losses](doc:battle#magic rule modifications) for both players, be very mindful of just trading resources.
- Using [treasures](doc:game-pieces#treasures) to win battles is much better in later stages than in early stages.
- If you have a repeatable [treasure](doc:game-pieces#treasures) producer, try to keep it alive while exhausting your opponents resources.

## Reward

- Be very careful which [upgrade](doc:game-pieces#upgrades) you choose. You don't need to apply it immediately, but the longer you wait, the weaker you are.
- Applying [upgrades](doc:game-pieces#upgrades) are permanent. So don't choose one that will go onto a card you don't plan on playing in the lategame.
