## Overview

**How many players can play?**
The game supports 2–8 players (must be even). Each round, players are paired up for battles.

**How long does a game take?**
A typical game takes 30–60 minutes online. In-person games take around 60–90 minutes.

**What happens when I lose a battle?**
You receive poison counters. The number depends on how many upgrades your opponent has (or the Fibonacci stage formula if upgrades are off). When your poison reaches the lethal threshold, you're eliminated.

**Can I concede a battle?**
No. Conceding is not allowed because treasures created during battle persist afterwards. You must play out the game.

**What are treasures for?**
Treasures are the economy of the game. Spend them during the draft to reroll your pack for better cards. You keep up to 5 treasures after each battle.

**How does play/draw work?**
The player with the most poison counters gets to choose play or draw. In the finals, the loser of the previous game chooses instead.

**What is a Vanquisher card?**
A special card you receive at the end of the third round of each stage. It increases your hand size by 1 for all future stages.

**Can I play this in paper?**
Yes! The game was designed to be played with physical Magic cards. Check out the [primer on CubeCobra](https://cubecobra.com/cube/about/auto?view=primer) for paper rules.
